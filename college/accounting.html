Computer Science Course

Description: Accounting is the systematic recording, analysis, and reporting of financial transactions and information. The course covers principles, concepts, and practices related to financial accounting, management accounting, and auditing. Students learn to prepare financial statements, analyze financial data, and apply accounting standards and regulations to ensure accuracy and transparency in financial reporting. Through case studies, simulations, and practical exercises, they develop proficiency in financial analysis, budgeting, cost management, and internal controls, essential for making informed business decisions and evaluating organizational performance. Accounting plays a crucial role in providing stakeholders with reliable financial information, facilitating investment decisions, and ensuring accountability and integrity in business operations.
